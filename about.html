Neoclassicism is both an artistic movement, and a stylistic period
which emerged around 1750 in the European Enlightenment era,
contemporary and subsequent to the popularity of the Grand Tour. Wikipedia

Neo-classical Refers to the Assimilation of Classical
Cultural Norms into the Modern World.


ÔLAB Group contributes to the assimilation of classical cultural
norms and forms into the modern world, with regard to traditions,
aesthetics, culture and art.

By creating highest quality products with visual effects that evoke
an image of elegance and romanticism, as well as a wonderful sensation
of touching the history and beauty of France. - In order to transmit
 the charm of France and its unique style, which is loved and
 appreciated around the World.

Etymology of "Classicism"

From the French word "Classique", derived with the
suffix - isme. Appeared in 1817 with the meaning of
"character of literary works that refer to ancient art"
and in 1875 with the meaning of "character of what is
harmonious, balanced"

Etymology of "Neoclassicism"

Derived from Classicism, with the prefix Neo-
